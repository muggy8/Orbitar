Orbitar: An Evolution in Space Tourism and Research

Orbitar is the revolutionary service that specializes in space tourism and research for all humans on Earth. The Orbitar Elevator will whisk you from Earth to our Orbitar Space Station comfortably, safely, and quickly, at an affordable cost. Once arriving at the space station, you can enjoy our vast amount of amenities and facilities, ranging from sightseeing and entertainment to academic research.

The Orbitar Corporation was founded in 2059 by Robert Hamilton, with our initial elevator and space station launched in 2064. With over 25 years of experience, we were the first company to take bring 10 million people into space.

Orbital Elevator

The orbital elevator will transport you from Earth to the space station, 45,000 kilometres above the planet, in less than 12 hours of ascend time. With a fleet of 4 elevators, and a capacity of 40 passengers per elevator, we make it easy for you to book a trip. Our Earth elevator base is located in Mombasa, Kenya.

Cost: $5800 per return trip

To continue booking your trip, please book your hotel accommodations and any optional room/equipment bookings below.

  • Lounge with recliners, televisions, wireless internet access, and large windows
  • Snacks and two meals included, with the ability to upgrade or purchase more meals on board
  • Shared sleeping rooms with comfortable beds, with harnesses available so you won't float out of bed when reaching space
  • Pressurized cabins and climate control provide a comfortable atmosphere
  • Spacious washrooms
  • Wheelchair accessible, including lifts to move between each floor

Orbitar Telescope

View the vast cosmos of the universe with our ultra-high definition telescope! Use our user-friendly computer system to adjust the telescope and its settings with precision, or use the optical viewfinder to view the universe live with your eyes. Take extremely high-resolution images as a keepsake or for research!

Cost: $150/hour, $3000/24 hour period, or $19,000/week

Visitors and scientists are both allowed to use the telescope.

  • Observation spectrum: infrared, optical, ultraviolet (limited)
  • Internal gyroscope
  • 90000x55000 resolution optical sensor
  • Spectrograph analyzer
  • Up to 240 hours of focus on one area (deep field observation permit required)
  • Gravitational lens correction
  • Up to 50,000,000x magnification
  • Up to 1,000,000x illumination

Astrochemistry Lab

Using the astrochemistry laboratory, scientists can conduct studies and experiments to understand chemical processes outside of Earth. Our lab has a variety of specialized instruments and chemicals to support research. The lab has an artificial gravity generator installed, so that you can safely and easily conducting experiments, similar to how they can be done as if you were on Earth.

Operational cost: $20 per hour, or $400 per 24 hours; additional chemical costs are extra.

Astrochemistry lab users must be affiliated with a professional research organization.

Particle Accelerator

The Orbitar Collider lets scientists conduct experiments involving particle physics, and how it is different while under the influence of microgravity.

Usage Cost: $250 per hour, or $5500 per 24 hours

Users of the Orbitar Collider must be affiliated with a professional research organization.

  • Proton/antiproton particle collision
  • Beam acceleration of up to 980 GeV
  • 6.3-kilometre circumference circular accelerator ring
  • 1275 sensors to measure collisions
  • Redundant safety sensors to detect and alert about any irregularities during operation, with automatic emergency safety shutoffs

Launch Pad

Our launch pad on board the space station allows scientists to launch and dock small space probes, satellites, and manned capsules. Its state of the art technology design allows for multiple launches per day. Bookings may be made while booking the trip, or while at the space station.

To use the launch pad, users must be affiliated with a professional research or engineering organization or be in the possession of a space launch permit.

  • Room size: 25 meters wide x 7.5 meters tall x 30 meters deep
  • Non-pressurized (space suit required)
  • 12 mechanical arms up to 15 meters long each
  • Observation deck for visitors to observe work within the pad
  • Unintended space drift protecting system
  • Unmanned rescue pods
  • Space launch support system
  • Space vehicle return guidance system
